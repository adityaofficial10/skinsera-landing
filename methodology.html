Methodology & Disclosures

Last updated: May 2026

How Skinsera's AI Skin Analysis Works

Skinsera uses a multi-step AI pipeline to generate skin appearance scores from face photos you submit:

  • Photo capture: You take a face photo using the app's guided camera interface, capturing front and side angles under stable lighting.
  • Image processing: Submitted photos are resized and normalized for consistent analysis across lighting conditions and device types.
  • AI vision analysis: Your photos are analyzed by a computer vision model (OpenAI's Vision model) that evaluates visible skin characteristics — including texture, evenness, redness, and surface quality — across different zones of your face.
  • Score generation: The AI outputs numerical scores (0–100) for each facial zone (forehead, left cheek, right cheek, chin, nose) and three sub-metrics: texture, clarity, and redness. An overall composite score is derived from these inputs.
  • Trend tracking: Over time, scores are compared against your historical baseline to identify patterns and surface AI-generated insights about potential correlations with your logged lifestyle factors.

What the Scores Mean

Scores range from 0 to 100 and reflect the AI's assessment of visible cosmetic appearance — not a medical or clinical evaluation of skin health.

  • Texture: Assesses smoothness vs. roughness based on visible surface quality.
  • Clarity: Measures evenness of skin tone and presence of dark spots, hyperpigmentation, or discoloration.
  • Redness: Evaluates visible erythema, irritation, or flushing-related signals.

Not a Medical Diagnosis: Skinsera provides cosmetic appearance analysis only. The scores and insights generated by the app do not constitute a medical, dermatological, or clinical assessment of your skin. Skinsera does not diagnose, treat, or offer advice on any skin disease, condition, or medical concern. For any medical skin concerns, please consult a qualified dermatologist or healthcare provider.

Data Used by the AI

Skinsera analyzes only:

  • Face photos you voluntarily submit through the app's scan feature
  • Metadata about those photos (such as capture quality signals for logging purposes)

Skinsera does not access medical records, HealthKit data, or any other personal health information for scoring purposes.

AI Accuracy & Limitations

  • Scores reflect visible appearance characteristics from photos, not a clinical skin examination. Lighting, camera quality, and photo angle can affect results.
  • Skinsera is not a substitute for professional skin analysis by a dermatologist.
  • The app is designed to help you track appearance trends over time, not to provide definitive assessments of skin health.
  • AI-generated insights about correlations with lifestyle factors are observational patterns, not causal medical findings.

AI Provider

Skinsera's skin scoring uses the OpenAI Vision API for image analysis, processed through secure Supabase Edge Functions. Your face photos are not used to train any AI model and are processed solely to generate your personal skin scores.

Skin Conditions We Track

Skinsera tracks the following visible cosmetic concerns, grounded in clinical literature:

  • Acne and breakouts
  • Oily or shiny skin
  • Dark spots and hyperpigmentation
  • Dark circles under the eyes
  • Dry or flaky skin
  • Keratosis pilaris (rough bumps)
  • Redness and rosacea-prone skin

Tracking is cosmetic and appearance-based, not diagnostic.
